ABC NOW

ABC NOW was started to help children learn the alphabet and start associating words with images. Simply built with toddlers in mind, let your child click each letter of the alphabet to reveal a word and image. You can focus on one letter at a time while building memory by repetition and using the fun activities included. Let’s learn the ABC’S!

Activities

Sing

Have fun with it! You can sing A-Z in different melodies and pronounce each letter strongly. Singing can be built into an existing routine like nap/bedtime, or even outside play (my daughter was especially fond of reciting the alphabet while jumping around and while relaxing in her swing). Once that becomes repetitive you can start singing a letter and word together, for example “A is for apple so tasty to eat” and continue through the alphabet.

Magnets

What toddler doesn’t love magnets? Using your refrigerator / dishwasher or anything else metal invest in a pack of alphabet and image magnets. You can set up the alphabet and then ask your child to find the image magnet that starts with a specific letter. You can also flip this activity around and choose an image magnet, talk to your child about that image and then ask them to find the letter that the image magnet starts with.

Flash Cards

You can find Preschool flashcards at most retailers and they will come with letters, images, and words. Using these in alphabetical order to start with for a few trial runs and then switching up the letter order will help your child build off the memorization they have already learned.
