Activities
  • Sing: Simple enough, integrate the alphabet into a song. You can sing A-Z in different melodies and pronounce each letter strongly. Singing can be built into an existing routine like nap/bedtime, or even outside play (my daughter was especially fond of reciting the alphabet while jumping around and while relaxing in her swing). Once that becomes repetitive you can sing a letter and word together, for example “A is for apple so tasty to eat” and continue through the alphabet.
  • Magnets:Using your refrigerator / dishwasher invest in some alphabet and image magnets. You can set up the alphabet and then ask your child to find the image magnet that starts with a specific letter. You can also flip this activity around and choose an image magnet, talk to your child about the image and then ask them to find the letter magnet.
  • Flash cards:You can find Preschool flashcards at most retailers and they will come with letters and image/words. Using these in alphabetical order to start with for a few trial runs and then switching up the letter order will help your child build off of the memorization they have already learned.
